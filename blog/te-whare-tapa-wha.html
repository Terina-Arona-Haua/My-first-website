Te Whare Tapa Whā is a model of Māori health and is based on the metaphor of a wharenui. It consists of four interconnected walls, or dimensions, of health: Taha Tinana (physical wellbeing), Taha Wairua (spiritual well being), Taha Hinengaro (mental and emotional wellbeing), and Taha Whānau (family and social well being). The model emphasises the importance of addressing and nurturing each of these in order to achieve overall wellbeing and balance.

Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing) Moving my body at least 30 minutes. Eating a balanced diet with plenty of fruits, vegetables, and whole grains. Getting at least 7-8 hours of sleep each night.
Taha Whānau (Family Wellbeing) Calling my parents at least once a week. Spending quality time with my family and friends. Practising active listening and open communication with my family and friends.
Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing) Meditating at least 10 minutes each day. Journaling and practising gratitude for at least 10 minutes each day. Seeking support when needed.
Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing) Meditating or practising mindfulness for at least 10 minutes each day. Connecting with nature by going for a hike or spending time outdoors once a week. Reading spiritual or philosophical texts to further my understanding of my personal beliefs and values.
Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment) Walking on the beach or in nature at least once a week Continuing to be environmentally conscious Continuing to educate myself on environmental issues
